Update on Exam Formats

Print this page
beginning of content:

Virtual reality exam worlds are available for every exam in May 2021.

In light of the challenges of remote learning during the 2020-2021 school year, the College Bored has created virtual reality worlds for all 36 subject exams this May. These immersive versions of the exams were designed to engage all learners.

We are excited about the new virtual reality exams because students who are more engaged were found to score higher. In the past, exams taken on paper or through traditional online forms could barely hold students attention for more than a few minutes. Students were dozing off during the 3-hour exam, which led to missed points.

Immersive exams are the best way for students to actively engage with each question. For history exams, they will walk through the past to interact with historical figures and feel the context of the moment. English exams will place students in word clouds, in the audiences of speeches, or in the setting of novels. Math exams will take place entirely inside a black board of a seasoned professor. The science exams will have students on the Magic School Bus, zooming through biomes, inside human body systems, and simulating the word problems of FRQs. Every exam will be an experience to remember, but can never be discussed on social media.

In an historic partnership with Facebook, Oculus Rift sets will be available for every student at the low cost of $750. Although this is a new required fee for every test taker, we at College Bored are dedicated to our mission of connecting all students to college success. Part of the college experience is to purchase expensive resources and this move will better equip students for the realities of post-secondary education in America.

The virtual reality exam will be available during any of the three exam administrations. Schools that offer it during the first administration will be required to provide a 6x6 ft space for students so they can openly explore the exam world. Students can also take the VR exams at home during the second or third administrations.

Proctors will be roaming the campus of the virtual world throughout all exams. We are committed to preserving the security and fairness of the exams, so the virtual proctors have been trained to scream at the top of their lungs if any cheating is detected. These screams can only be heard by the individual test-taker, but will shake them to the core.

We've made these decisions to prioritize the health and safety of educators and students during these unprecedented times. We believe this new approach will best serve students and educators, balance course breadth and depth, and honor the full, essential story of Exams.
